Now

I won't exaggerate by saying that the time spent in my high school (Ayb High School) was by far the best and most memorable period of my life.

Ayb gave me the opportunity to rediscover old hobbies, find new ones, and get used to stepping out of my comfort zone.

Fun Fact: I was a finalist of the first season of the tv show The Brainiest (Armenian adaptation of the widely known British TV quiz show Britain's Brainiest Kid)

Army service in Armenia is mandatory, and it lasts for two years. Army days have been the most life-changing and challenging period of my life.

Since I had completed my secondary education and was already a university student, I had the chance to get recruited to a military training base before moving to my main one.

After receiving training in anti-aircraft warfare, I continued the rest of my service in the main base. Various events and experiences (including the Second Nagorno-Karabagh War) led to a personal paradigm shift in worldview, work ethic and values.

As I've mentioned, before starting my army service, I actually got accepted to the American University of Armenia (accredited by the WASC Senior College and University Commission) and completed my freshman year as a BA in Business student. After returning from the army, I continued with my sophomore year.

Another Fun Fact: During the 6th Starmus Festival in Armenia - I met the Canadian astronaut and musician Chris Hadfield.

As satisfied as I was with my education, I decided to take a gap year to focus entirely on myself, learn new things, and develop as an invidual.

My love for AI (combined with my knowledge of finance gained in AUA) led me to study programming and dive deeper into data science and machine learning in finance and investments.
